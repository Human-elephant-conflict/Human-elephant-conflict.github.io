About Me

I am Talia, a student from Shanghai. During the summer of 10th grade, I came across the topic of the human-elephant conflict in Yunnan and conducted field research there. In Dashujiao Village, I learned about the difficulties faced by local residents coexisting with elephants, which inspired me to create this website to raise awareness of the issue. As I mentioned on the solution page, this is a very complex issue. Although my ability is limited as an individual, I hope more people will pay attention to this problem and become aware of it so that new solutions may emerge.

My Research

When I arrived at Dashujiao Village and saw the damage to the villagers' fields and houses, I hoped to find methods that directly prevent elephants from invading their properties. This led to the following two articles.

Electric Fences

This article analyzes the characteristics of electric fences and elephant behavior, concluding that the effectiveness of electric fences declines as elephants have more knowledge about the fences.

Warning System

This article introduces the local warning systems in Yunnan. While the system is effective, it cannot fundamentally resolve the conflict, serving only as a preventive measure.

Fundraising

During my field research, I also discovered that some elderly people, who were in poor health and had difficult financial situations, were threatened by the elephants invasion. As a result, I initiated a fundraising to purchase electric fences in the hope of reducing the threat elephants posed to their safety.

2022 Fundraising

Electric fences were purchased for six households, raising a total of 5,341 yuan.

2023 Fundraising

An electric fence was purchased for one household, raising a total of 1,596 yuan.

Supporting Local Farmers

In the hopes of improving the economic situation of local tea farmers, we helped the farmers design a brand called "Yunnan Elephant Tea". With the unique culture behind the tea, it allows the farmers to sell more tea to sustain their life.
